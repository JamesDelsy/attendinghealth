Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus: Outpatient Management

Overview

Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell dysfunction. Outpatient management centers on glycemic control, cardiovascular risk reduction, and prevention of microvascular complications.

First-line pharmacotherapy has shifted toward agents with demonstrated cardiorenal benefit, independent of glycemic effect.

Diagnosis

  • Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL on two separate occasions
  • HbA1c ≥6.5%, measured by an NGSP-certified assay
  • 2-hour plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL during a 75g OGTT
  • Random plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL with classic hyperglycemic symptoms

Treatment Algorithms

Bottom-line recommendation Level A Evidence

Initiate Metformin 500–2000mg/day, titrated over 4–8 weeks, alongside lifestyle intervention. Add a GLP-1 receptor agonist or SGLT2 inhibitor for patients with established cardiovascular or renal disease, regardless of baseline HbA1c.

Its mechanism — primarily hepatic gluconeogenesis suppression — complements the extra-pancreatic actions of incretin-based therapies.

Complications

  • Diabetic retinopathy — annual dilated eye exam
  • Diabetic nephropathy — annual urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio
  • Peripheral neuropathy — annual monofilament foot exam
Critical interaction

Concurrent use with iodinated contrast media requires temporary metformin discontinuation due to risk of lactic acidosis in patients with impaired renal clearance.
